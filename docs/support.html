AnyListen — Support

AnyListen routes a live microphone — the built-in mic, a USB interface, a headset, or Bluetooth — straight to your headphones or hearing aids in real time. All audio stays on your device; see the Privacy Policy.

Contact us

For help, bug reports, or feature requests, open an issue on the AnyListen GitHub repository. We respond there — it's the fastest way to reach the developer.

Common questions

Why does the app say "Connect headphones"?
Listening always requires headphones (or another external output such as hearing aids or USB audio). Routing a live microphone to the iPhone speaker would cause instant feedback, so the speaker route is blocked on purpose.

Why is the Listen button disabled?
Either the speaker is the selected output (see above), or the microphone you chose isn't connected. Reconnect the device or pick another input with the Change button.

Why does nothing happen when I tap Start Listening?
Microphone access was probably denied. Open iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone and turn AnyListen on. The app also shows an "Open Settings" shortcut when microphone access is off.

Does AnyListen record or upload my audio?
No. Audio is passed through in real time only — never recorded, never stored, never sent anywhere.
